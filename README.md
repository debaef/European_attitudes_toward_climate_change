## Key Insights

### üåç Levels of Concern Across Europe
The analysis shows that climate change was already perceived as a significant issue across Europe in 2016‚Äì2017.  
Southern European countries, in particular, tend to express **higher levels of concern**, likely due to their direct exposure to climate-related phenomena such as:
- Heatwaves  
- Droughts  
- Wildfires  
- Water scarcity  

These daily experiences appear to strongly influence public perception.

---

### Germany as a Standout Case
Germany emerges as a particularly interesting case.  
Despite not being part of Southern Europe, it shows a **high level of perceived responsibility** toward climate change.

- **Average responsibility index:** **6.59 / 10**
- **Ranking:** Second only to France

This strong sense of responsibility is likely connected to Germany‚Äôs policy framework, particularly the **Climate Action Plan 2050**, which set long-term emission reduction targets across all sectors.  
The combination of policy ambition and accountability mechanisms seems to reinforce public awareness and responsibility.

---

### ‚ö° Energy Source Preferences
At a European level, the data reveals that citizens show **very close average preferences** between:
- Renewable energy sources  
- Non-renewable energy sources  

This suggests that, during the 2016‚Äì2017 period, Europe was in a **transitional phase**, with growing support for renewables but without a clear and uniform dominance across all countries.
